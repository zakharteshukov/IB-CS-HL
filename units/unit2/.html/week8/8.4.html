Introduction

Learning Objectives:

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to: 

  • Apply abstraction, decomposition, pattern recognition, and algorithmic design to solve a structured real-world problem. 
  • Identify how the four computational thinking concepts work together. 
  • Document and justify your reasoning at each stage of problem-solving. 

Introduction 

In the previous lesson, we explored the core concepts of computational thinking. Today, we will use those concepts to solve a real-world problem. 

Computational thinking is not just a set of isolated skills - it is most powerful when these skills are used together.  In this lesson, you will work through a scenario and learn how to: 

  • Simplify the problem by focusing on what matters (abstraction) 
  • Break the problem into smaller, manageable parts (decomposition) 
  • Identify familiar patterns that can guide your thinking (pattern recognition) 
  • Design a step-by-step plan to solve it (algorithmic design) 

Real-World Scenario

Organizing a School Coding Hackathon 

You will be working individually for this activity. Imagine you are part of the student tech club responsible for organizing a 1-day coding hackathon at your school. Students from different grades will participate in teams. You will need to handle the following five tasks: 

  • Registration of participants 
  • Allocation of teams 
  • Venue setup and schedule 
  • Mentorship and judging 
  • Final award ceremony 

 

Below, we provide an example solution using the four-column computational thinking framework for the task:

 

Registration of participants

Concept  What You Need to Consider  Examples from the Scenario  Example Strategy  
Abstraction  What details can be ignored? What core information matters most?  Ignore decorative preferences, long bios; focus on name, grade, contact info, dietary needs  Only ask for necessary details like name, grade, emergency contact, team preference (if any). Use a simple form to avoid confusion. 
Decomposition  What smaller tasks make up the larger task?  Form design, data collection, data verification, confirmation email, deadline tracking  Design registration form → Share via email and posters → Collect responses → Confirm registration via automated email. 
Pattern Recognition  Have you seen similar school processes before? What worked? What didn’t?  Previous club sign-ups, science fair registration forms  Use familiar digital tools like Google Forms and email filters to streamline. Avoid open-ended inputs to prevent inconsistency. 
Algorithmic Design  What sequence of steps or rules would ensure smooth execution of the registration process?  Form creation → Promotion → Collection → Sorting → Confirmation  Step 1: Create Google Form. Step 2: Share link and QR code. Step 3: Track submissions. Step 4: Sort into database. Step 5: Send confirmation messages.
